# Early Career — Tello Corp, Comdial, Techna International, PriceWaterhouseCoopers

## Overview
From 2000 to 2006, Sobhan Dutta served as a UI designer and developer across multiple companies: Tello Corp, Comdial, Techna International, and PriceWaterhouseCoopers. During this period he led end-to-end interface design, frontend development, and interaction design across web, mobile, and desktop platforms.

## Tello Corp
At Tello Corp, Sobhan worked on ZCR (Zero Cost Routing), a directory-based IP calling service. He also built cross-platform clients supporting real-time updates. This work gave him early exposure to network technology products and the challenge of designing intuitive interfaces for complex telecommunications systems.

## Comdial
At Comdial, Sobhan worked on telecommunications and communication platform UX, contributing to enterprise communication systems during the early era of internet telephony.

## Techna International
At Techna International, Sobhan gained experience in international software development, building interfaces for global enterprise clients and developing an understanding of cross-cultural design considerations.

## PriceWaterhouseCoopers (PwC)
At PricewaterhouseCoopers, Sobhan contributed to enterprise software and consulting projects, working alongside business and technology teams to deliver client-facing tools and internal platforms.

## Key Skills Developed in Early Career
- End-to-end interface design across web, mobile, and desktop
- Frontend development using the technologies of the era (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery)
- Interaction design and user experience principles
- Real-time application development
- Cross-platform development

## Significance
This early career period established Sobhan's foundation as both a designer and engineer — a combination that has defined his career. Working across diverse companies in different sectors gave him exposure to a wide range of industries and product types, forming the broad perspective he brings to complex enterprise UX challenges.
