# Principal Engineer – UX/UI Architecture — Nuance (Microsoft)

## Role Overview
Sobhan Dutta served as Principal Engineer – UX/UI Architecture at Nuance Communications in Sunnyvale, CA from 2006 to 2020, spanning 14 years. Nuance was later acquired by Microsoft. During his tenure, Sobhan led UX strategy, architecture, and frontend development for multiple enterprise products across IVR, analytics, and big-data visualization.

Nuance's products were used by Fortune 500 clients and global telecom providers. Sobhan worked across design, product, and engineering to deliver customer-centric, scalable solutions in a fast-paced, acquisition-driven environment that evolved from BeVocal to Nuance to Microsoft.

## Key Responsibilities
- Defining and executing UX strategy across a portfolio of analytics and infrastructure tools
- Architecting frontend systems for enterprise-scale IVR, analytics, and big-data visualization platforms
- Collaborating with PMs, customers, and offshore teams to validate concepts via prototypes and user research
- Leading offshore development and design teams across distributed environments
- Building 0-to-1 products from concept through production launch
- Delivering multi-million dollar ARR products used by Fortune 500 clients

## Products Launched at Nuance
- **IVR Insights**: Analytics platform for IVR (Interactive Voice Response) systems used by Fortune 500 companies
- **Nuance Application Viewer**: Enterprise visualization tool for IVR application flows
- **Unified Application Frameworks**: Cross-platform frontend architecture framework
- **DNM (Dial Number Manager)**: 0-to-1 product for managing dial number configurations
- **CS (Configuration Services)**: Enterprise configuration management tool
- **ODI (On-Demand Insight)**: Real-time analytics and insight platform
