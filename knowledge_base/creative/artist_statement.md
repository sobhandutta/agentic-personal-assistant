## Statement

My work explores spaces filled with energy, centered on abstract forms and shapes, often created from everyday disposable materials such as paper, foam, fabric, paper towels, and wood. By using humble materials in unexpected ways, I seek to make the medium itself independent and expressive.

I experiment with abstract forms and techniques to represent emotions and perceptions through imagined characters, portraying them as a union of reality and imagination. My works are often collaged into spatial compositions where energy, movement, and stillness coexist.

Stylized forms, flowing colors, volume, and the fluidity of lines and strokes help depict sensuality, motion, and calm within the same visual language. Through warm hues, vibrant colors, and the juxtaposition of abstraction, representation, and stylized imagery—grounded in academic technique—I strive to create a unique place within contemporary art.

The soulfulness of mood and memory, sweeping gestures of color, transparent layers, and tactile textures all contribute to what I hope becomes a coalescence of art and thought.

From a very early age, I drew pictures for the sheer joy of it. I was more interested in collecting images from master painters to recreate them, and in making clay models of goddesses, than in studying. My school textbooks were filled with drawings—I illuminated chapter headings and covered pages with faces and objects. I drew what I imagined, and it felt magical. The power to create beautiful things that did not exist in ordinary life fascinated me.

By high school, I began receiving recognition, and when asked what I wanted to be when I grew up, the most natural answer was always: *I’ll be an artist.*

Born in India and living in the United States for over twenty years, I received a Bachelor of Visual Arts from Government College of Art & Craft and a Master of Visual Arts from Rabindra Bharati University. I now live and work in the San Francisco Bay Area, California.
