# Sobhan Dutta — Creative Work (Painting & Sculpture)

## Artist Statement

My work is involved with space, filled with energy. To make the medium completely independent I sometimes experiment with these forms and techniques to realize my emotions and perceptions in the characters while portraying as a union of reality and imagination.

My works are based mostly on abstract forms and shapes collaged into space. My materials are made mostly from daily used disposable items — paper, foam, fabrics, paper towels, wood, etc.

Stylized forms, flowing colors, volume to the shapes, and the fluidity of lines and strokes depict sensuality, movement of forms, and stillness — all co-existing in my work. Using warm hues and vibrant colors and the juxtapose of the abstract, representative, and stylized art, topped with academic technique, I have tried to create a unique place within the art scene. The soulfulness of mood and memory, sweep and slash of colors, the transparent depth of colors, and texture are all that make my artistry a wonderful coalescence of art and thought.

## Background

From a very early age I drew pictures for the sheer joy of it. I was more interested in collecting images from master painters, to recreate them and create clay models for goddess rather than studying. In school, my textbooks were filled with drawings; I illuminated chapter headings and drew faces and objects all over the pages. I drew whatever I saw in my mind and it seemed magical. The power to make beautiful things that did not exist in real life was my fascination. By the time I reached high school, I started getting recognition, and when asked what I wanted to be when I "grew up," the most natural answer I provided was "I'll be an artist."

Born in India and moved to the USA about 20 years back. I received my bachelor's from Govt. College of Art and Craft and a master's degree in visual arts from Rabindra Bharati University. I now live and work in the San Francisco Bay Area, California.

## Project 365: Coffee with Sketch

**Platform:** Facebook  
**URL:** https://www.facebook.com/CoffeeAndSketch

### Overview
An ongoing artistic initiative following strict self-imposed discipline: one sketch created and uploaded every day during a coffee break, with each piece completed in approximately 10 minutes.

The artwork frequently features abstract and expressive portraiture, often utilizing circular motifs that resemble coffee stains or cup rings. The project captures both the practice of daily art-making and the beauty of ephemeral expression — themes of discipline, spontaneity, and creative exploration.

### Themes
- Minimalist and experimental sketches (sometimes using coffee itself as a medium)
- Disposability, obligation, and fulfillment
- Abstract and expressive portraiture
- Daily practice as artistic discipline
